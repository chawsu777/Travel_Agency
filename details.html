Tour Details

Seoul, the capital of South Korea, is a huge metropolis where modern skyscrapers, high-tech subways and pop culture meet Buddhist temples, palaces, street markets and for being the birthplace of K-pop and Hallyu. Seoul is known for its vibrant districts, eclectic fashion scene and delicious street food.

N Seoul Tower

Everyone visiting Seoul should go up to the city’s iconic observation tower. Its position is almost on top of a mountain. There are miles of hiking trails to explore. The top levels of the N Seoul Tower include indoor and outdoor areas and restaurants. The exterior of the tower is covered in LED lighting. There is a cable car that takes you from the city to the tower’s base area. You can then hike from there. This place has also become a popular date spot for couples, which is why it is perfect for White Day. Like the Pont Neuf bridge in Paris, couples, families, friends buy and seal a lock onto a railing or designated area of the Tower in hopes of an everlasting love.

Changdeokgung Palace

Changdeokgung Palace is the second World Heritage Site in Seoul. It was selected as a representative palace for its notable beauty in the history of place architecture in East Asia and its excellent arrangement with the surrounding natural environment. Changdeokgung Palace was built by King Taejong in 1405. This 600-year-old UNESCO World Heritage Site is the city's jewel. It's as famous for its outstanding architectural details as for its verdant and expansive gardens.

Bukchon Hanok Village

Bukchon Hanok Village is one of the most beautiful ancient villages in Seoul, South Korea. This dreamy village was built 600 years ago, and it still retains its beauty now. Bukchon Hanok Village has a lot of cultural centers and museums that showcase the culture of the Joseon Dynasty. It's a unique display of the dynasty's architecture and way of life.

Seoul’s Street Food Market

As one of the world’s great food cultures, no visit to Korea is complete without enjoying some street foods. The Gwangjang Market, in Central Seoul, is truly a foodie heaven on Earth. The large covered market area is filled with multiple food stalls offering the complete array of Korean specialities. The cool thing about the market is that the food stalls are like mini restaurants, in that they have a row of stools and a counter, so you can sit and eat.
